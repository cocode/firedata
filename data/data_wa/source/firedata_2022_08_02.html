Incident Information -- Northwest Large Fire Information Summary

A Large Fire as defined by the National Wildland Coordinating Group, is any wildland fire in timber 100 acres or greater, and 300 acres or greater in grasslands/rangelands, or has an Incident Management Team assigned to it.

Information received from the National ICS-209 incident reporting database for large fires is updated each morning, and shows what has been reported by the Incident Commander the previous day at 7:00 PM. Information shown for each fire incident on the current day may not reflect changes made since the previous days ICS-209 input.

Status

BAKER CITY, Ore. — After working through the night Sunday, firefighting crews were working this
morning to secure the perimeter of the Big Rattlesnake Fire 11 miles southwest of Medical Springs, Ore.
The lightning-caused fire is burning on private property and public lands protected by Vale Bureau of Land
Management. It was reported about 5:30 p.m. Sunday. Aerial mapping this morning showed it to be 425 acres.
Baker County Sheriff’s Office has lowered the evacuation order for residents west of Hwy. 203 to Level 1 – Be
Ready – and lifted the order for residents east of Hwy. 203 between Miles Bridge Road and Medical Springs.
Hwy. 203 is closed to all traffic except emergency vehicles and local residents.
“We had no new growth as of this morning, Vale BLM Fire Duty Officer Jonathan Dunbar said. “Crews will
continue to secure the perimeter and mop up today.”
Three Single Engine Air Tankers made retardant drops before it became too dark to fly Sunday. This morning,
six engines were on scene and two hand crews were en route. A bulldozer was cutting line on private property.
The BLM-managed portion of the fire is located in the 5,880-acre Powder River Canyon Area of Critical
Environmental Concern, which includes the 2,385-acre Powder Wild and Scenic River and the five-pasture Big
Creek Allotment.
It is managed to protect raptor and wildlife habitat, cultural resources and scenic qualities while allowing for
compatible recreational uses. Long before the arrival of pioneers and settlers, the Cayuse, Umatilla, and Nez
Perce Indians utilized the hunting and fishing grounds along the length of the Powder River.
Sage-grouse habitat is among the values at risk. The fire is burning in heavy sagebrush along the drainages and
lighter fuels on ridgetops.
Local resources responding include Medical Springs Rural Fire Protection District, North Powder Rural Fire
Protection District, Baker Rural Fire Protection District, Lookout-Glasgow Rangeland Fire Protection
Association, Eagle Valley Rural Fire Protection District and Keating Rural Fire Protection District.

Roseburg, OR - Suppression efforts are underway for the Windigo Fire, which began Saturday afternoon in the Windigo Pass area off Forest
Service (FS) Road 60 on the Douglas and Klamath county divide. Ground and aviation resources responded quickly to the fire which is
actively burning in timber. Today’s efforts continued focus on identifying and establishing primary containment line as part of the full
suppression response to the fire. A Type 2 Incident Management Team arrived today to manage this fire and the Potter fire to the north on
the Willamette National Forest. There is poor visibility from California wildfire smoke and a Red Flag Warning for lightning will be in effect from
Noon today through 11:00 p.m.

Multiple closures of roads, trails, and campgrounds for public and firefighter safety
remain in place. Monitor the Forest website for updated closure information.
